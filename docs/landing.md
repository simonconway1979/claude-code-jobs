# Claude Code Jobs

**The community's own read on the Claude Code job market — the few roles worth acting on, surfaced by the people the market is actually chasing.**

A small team of us is building it in the open, and you can use it, build it, or just watch it happen. Reply in the channel or DM me and I'll add you to the repo.

---

## The problem

A job market is hard to read from the inside. You get fifty postings a night and spend the evening filtering instead of acting, "AI-native" is on every other listing whether it's true or not, pay stays hidden until late, and the genuinely good roles close in days rather than the weeks you'd assume. Generic job boards make this worse, because they can't tell a real AI-native role from a relabelled one. You can.

## What you get

A tool that pulls the fresh postings every evening, drops anything you've already seen, and screens what's left against criteria you wrote yourself, handing you a short ranked list where each role is marked GO, EXPLORE, or PASS with a line on why. The point isn't more jobs, it's spending your evening on the three that matter rather than the fifty that don't.

What makes the screen worth trusting is that it reads the actual job description rather than the tags, so it catches the office-days requirement and the country-locked "remote" that quietly sink applications, and it shows its reasoning on every call so you can argue with it instead of trusting a number. When a posting doesn't say something, it flags the gap and asks rather than guessing.

## Why this is worth building together

**For you,** it's a sharper search or a sharper hiring lens, tuned to your judgment and getting better each time you correct it.

**For the community,** it becomes something no board has: an honest signal on pay, a read on which companies genuinely work AI-native versus the ones who just say so, warm routes into roles through people you already know, and a sense of which skills are climbing. That only exists if the people living the market build it, which is exactly who's here.

**For the community's leaders,** it's a repeatable engine. If a small team can build something genuinely useful in the open that the wider community actually uses, then you've got a shape you can point at the next idea, and a way to turn the community's energy into things worth having without it becoming one person's unpaid second job.

So jobs is just the first one, because it's useful and it starts warm, but the shape underneath works for whatever the community needs next.

## How it works

You don't need a meeting to take part. The whole project lives in one shared repo, so when you open it your Claude already knows what it is and where it's up to, and you contribute by opening a pull request, whether that's feedback, an idea, or a half-built feature.

1. **Join** — reply or DM me and I'll add you to the repo.
2. **Get oriented** — clone it, run one command, and ask Claude what's here. It takes minutes.
3. **Put something in** — test the tool and feed back, fix a gap, or build a piece that interests you, then save it as a PR.

## What it could become is up to you

There's no roadmap here on purpose, because the most useful version is the one shaped by what you'd actually want from it. People have wondered aloud about tracking how the market shifts over time, getting an honest read on pay, telling the real AI-native shops from the pretenders, or routing warm intros through the community, but those are hunches rather than plans, and the good ideas are the ones you bring.

## One more thing, if how teams work is your thing

Building this together is also a live experiment in AI-native team working, with shared context instead of standups, everything async, and a build-then-validate-then-kill rhythm where most of what gets made gets cut on purpose and in public. That's a real benefit and it's the reason I'm personally investing the time, but it sits behind the value above rather than leading it, because nobody should work on a project for the methodology. If it interests you, there's a whole doc on it once you're in.
