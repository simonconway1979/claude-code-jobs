# Why this matters

Start with the value, because nobody should spend their evenings on a project that doesn't drive any.

## The project

A job market is hard to read from the inside. The postings are noisy, "AI-native" is on every other listing whether it's true or not, pay is hidden until late, and the genuinely good roles move fast. This project is the community building its own clear read on the Claude Code job market — which roles are real, what they pay, what they actually want, and how it's all moving — starting from a tool that screens the daily firehose down to the few roles worth acting on.

## What's in it for you

If you're searching, or you might be soon, you get the handful of roles worth your evening instead of the fifty you'd never take, screened against your own judgment, with the reasoning shown so you can trust it or argue with it. That alone is worth having, and [what-it-does.md](../docs/what-it-does.md) covers it.

The bigger value, though, comes from *this* community being the one that builds it. A generic board can't tell a real AI-native role from a relabelled one, and you can. So the read you get back isn't a scrape, it's a market seen by the people the market is actually chasing.

## What's in it for the community

Put the community's knowledge in one place and it becomes something no board has: an honest signal on pay, a sense of which companies genuinely work AI-native versus the ones who just say so, warm routes into roles through people you already know, a read on which skills are climbing. None of that exists as a product anywhere, and this community is uniquely able to produce it because it lives the thing it's describing.

## What's in it for community leaders

A repeatable engine. If this works — a small team builds something genuinely useful, in the open, that the wider community actually uses — then you've got a proven shape you can point at the next idea. The value isn't only a jobs tool; it's a demonstrated way to turn the community's own energy into things worth having, without it becoming one person's unpaid second job.

## The bigger pattern: spin it up for the next idea

Jobs is the first one because it's useful and it starts warm, but the shape underneath — a small team builds a real thing together, in the open, and brings the value back to everyone — works for whatever the community needs next. So this is as much a template as it is a product. Get it right once and you've got a way to do it again.

## What it could become — your call, not mine

This part deliberately stays open. The most useful version is the one shaped by what you'd actually want, so rather than a roadmap, a few questions worth sitting with: What would make this genuinely useful to *you*, in your own search or when you're the one hiring? What does this community know collectively that no job board does, and how would you get it out of people's heads? If you could open "the Claude Code job market" tomorrow and act on it, what would it show you? People have wondered aloud about tracking how the market shifts, honest pay signal, telling the real AI-native shops from the pretenders, routing warm intros — but those are hunches, and the good ideas are the ones you bring. Drop one in `context/ideas/` or open a PR; a sentence that starts "I'd love it if this could..." counts.

## And a second benefit, if how teams work is your thing

Building this together is also a live experiment in AI-native team working — shared context, async, build then validate then kill. That's a genuine benefit, and it's the reason I'm personally investing the time, but it isn't why you'd use the tool, so it sits behind the value above. If it interests you, [ways-of-working.md](../context/ways-of-working.md) has it.
