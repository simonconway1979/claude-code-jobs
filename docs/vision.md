# Why this matters

Start with the value, because nobody should spend their evenings on a project that doesn't drive any.

## The project

A job market is hard to read from the inside. The postings are noisy, "AI-native" is on every other listing whether it's true or not, pay is hidden until late, the genuinely good roles move fast, and each of us only sees the sliver that crosses our own feed. This project is the community building its own clear read on the Claude Code job market: which roles are real, who's genuinely hiring for these skills, what they pay, what they actually want, and how it's all moving. It starts from a simple job screener I'm throwing into the pot because I have it and I'm happy to share, but that's a starting point, not the shape. The market read is the thing, and it's broad enough that everyone will want something different from it.

And "job" is the loosest word here. Some people in this community want employment. Plenty are freelance and chasing contracts. Some are building small businesses, some are aiming at the big corps, and some just need to find collaborators for a project. Those look like different goals, but they all rest on the same thing: reading the same market well. The value is painting that one picture from all those angles at once, so whichever you're after, the read has a side that's pointed at you.

## What's in it for you

There's no single user here. You bring the question and the same read answers it: if you're searching, the handful of roles worth your evening instead of the fifty you'd never take; if you're hiring, what good roles pay and who's around; if you're freelancing, where the demand is and what it's charging; if you're weighing whether to lean in, which skills are climbing. One read, built to serve more than one.

The bigger value, though, comes from *this* community being the one that builds it. A generic board can't tell a real AI-native role from a relabelled one, and you can. So the read you get back isn't a scrape, it's a market seen by the people the market is actually chasing.

## A starting point: the find-job screener

We didn't start from a blank page, and that's the point. I've just begun my own job search, so I already had a simple screener: it pulls the day's postings, drops the ones I've seen, and screens the rest against criteria I wrote myself, handing back a short ranked list with the reasoning shown. It runs today, it's genuinely useful, and it's almost certainly useful to others in the same spot.

So that's why we started with jobs, and why now: I'm looking, it likely helps people like me, and (the part that matters most) it's something real to build from rather than a plan on a whiteboard. It's one lens (a job seeker's filter) on the wider market read, not the whole thing. [what-it-does.md](../docs/what-it-does.md) covers exactly what it does; [quickstart.md](../docs/quickstart.md) runs it for your own search.

## What's in it for the community

Put the community's knowledge in one place and it becomes something no board has: an honest signal on pay, a sense of which companies genuinely work AI-native versus the ones who just say so, warm routes into roles through people you already know, a read on which skills are climbing. None of that exists as a product anywhere, and this community is uniquely able to produce it because it lives the thing it's describing.

## A pattern the community can reuse

The other thing the community gets is the *shape*. Jobs is the first one because it's useful and it starts warm, but underneath it is a repeatable engine: a small team builds a real thing together, in the open, and brings the value back to everyone, without it becoming one person's unpaid second job. Get that right once and you've got a proven way to turn the community's own energy into things worth having, ready to point at whatever it needs next. This is as much a template as it is a product.

## What it could become: your call, not mine

This part deliberately stays open. The most useful version is the one shaped by what you'd actually want, so rather than a roadmap, a few questions worth sitting with: What would make this genuinely useful to *you*, in your own search or when you're the one hiring? What does this community know collectively that no job board does, and how would you get it out of people's heads? If you could open "the Claude Code job market" tomorrow and act on it, what would it show you? People have wondered aloud about tracking how the market shifts, honest pay signal, telling the real AI-native shops from the pretenders, routing warm intros, but those are hunches, and the good ideas are the ones you bring. Drop one in `context/ideas/` or open a PR; a sentence that starts "I'd love it if this could..." counts.

## And a second benefit, if how teams work is your thing

Building this together is also a live experiment in AI-native team working: shared context, async, build then validate then kill. That's a genuine benefit, and it's the reason I'm personally investing the time, but it isn't why you'd use the tool, so it sits behind the value above. If it interests you, [ways-of-working.md](../context/ways-of-working.md) has it.
