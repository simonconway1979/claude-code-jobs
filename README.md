## The thing we're building

Right now nobody has a clear picture of the Claude Code job market. Postings are noisy and "AI-native" is on half of them whether it's true or not; pay stays hidden; the real roles move fast; and each of us only ever sees the sliver that crosses our own feed. Generic boards can't fix this: they can't tell a real AI-native role from a relabelled one, and they have no idea what this community knows.

We can. The goal is a shared, living read on the market (which roles are real, who's genuinely hiring for Claude Code skills, what they pay, what they actually want, which skills are climbing, and how all of it is moving), produced by the people living inside it.

## Why it's interesting to everyone, even though everyone wants something different

There's no single "user" here, and that's the point. The same underlying read serves very different questions:

- **Searching, or might be soon?** A sharp, honest shortlist instead of a fifty-a-night firehose, and a feel for which "AI-native" roles are the real thing.
- **Hiring?** A read on what good Claude Code roles look like, what they pay to stay competitive, and who's around.
- **Freelancing or consulting?** Where the demand actually is and what it's paying, so you know what to chase and what to charge.
- **Building something, or looking for people?** Whether you're starting a business, hiring into a small team, or just trying to find collaborators who actually know Claude Code, the same read shows you where they are and what they want.
- **Deciding whether to lean in?** Whether this market is growing, which skills are rising, and what's worth learning next: a read on the wave before you commit to riding it.
- **Just curious?** A front-row view of a brand-new job market forming in real time.

You don't have to care about all of those. You probably care a lot about one. The bet is that the same shared foundation can answer all of them, because it's built by people who can tell signal from noise.

## Where we start

I've just kicked off my own job search, so I built a simple but effective screener: it pulls the fresh postings each evening, drops what you've already seen, and screens the rest against criteria you wrote yourself, handing back a short ranked list, each role marked GO / EXPLORE / PASS with a line on why and the reasoning shown so you can argue with it. [what-it-does.md](docs/what-it-does.md) covers it; [quickstart.md](docs/quickstart.md) runs it for your own search.

That's on the table because I have it and I'm happy to share it, not because it's the plan. It's one lens (a filter for a job seeker) on a market that has many. Use it as-is, sharpen it, or ignore it and build the lens you actually want.

## Bring what you've already got

If you've built something that touches this (a tracker, a pay analysis, a scraper, a skills-trend thing, an alerting setup, anything), bring it. Part of the value of doing this as a community is pooling what people have already made separately and finding the best of it.

This is also something Claude is genuinely good at: take several people's takes on the same problem and surface what works in each. And if two people turn out to have built something similar, that overlap is itself a strong signal about what the market needs.

## How to take part

The whole project lives in one shared repo, so when you open it your Claude already knows what it is and where it's up to. You contribute by opening a pull request: feedback, an idea, a dataset, a half-built feature, or something finished you're willing to share.

1. **Join.** Reply or DM me and I'll add you to the repo.
2. **Get oriented.** Clone it, run one command, ask Claude what's here. Minutes.
3. **Put something in.** Use the screener and feed back, bring a tool you've built, or build a piece in the direction that's useful to you, then save it as a PR.

## Where it goes is unconstrained, on purpose

There's no roadmap, because the most useful version is the one shaped by what you'd actually want from the market read. In the build club people take it wherever it's useful to them. People have wondered aloud about tracking how the market shifts over time, an honest pay signal, telling the real AI-native shops from the pretenders, routing warm intros through the community, mapping which skills are rising. But those are prompts, not plans. The good directions are the ones you bring.

## One more thing, if how teams work is your thing

Building this together is also a live experiment in AI-native team working: shared context instead of standups, everything async, and a build-then-validate-then-kill rhythm where most of what gets made gets cut on purpose and in public. That's a real benefit and a big part of why I'm personally investing the time, but it sits behind the value above, because nobody should work on a project for the methodology. If it interests you, there's a whole doc on it once you're in.
