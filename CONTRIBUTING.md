# Contributing

Everything here arrives as a pull request: feedback, ideas, prior work, docs, code, half-built things. This page is the one thing to read before you contribute: who can use what you put in, and what you keep.

## The deal, in plain terms

**You keep your work. The community gets a permanent right to use it.**

When you open a PR, you licence your contribution to the project under the licences below, irrevocably and non-exclusively. "Irrevocably" means once it's in, it stays usable by everyone; you can't pull it back out later. "Non-exclusively" means **you keep your copyright and you can reuse your own contribution however you like**: in your own job search, your portfolio, another project, anything. Sharing it here doesn't sign it away.

We don't take ownership of your work, and you don't get to revoke the community's use of it once it's merged. Both at once. That's the whole norm.

**Your name stays on it.** Attribution is guaranteed: git history and the build log carry it. "Belongs to the community" never means anonymous.

## Only contribute what's yours to give

You can't licence what you don't own. So:

- **No third-party material you don't have rights to**: someone else's code, scraped datasets, paywalled or copyrighted text, an employer's internal tool.
- **No personal or confidential data**: real postings, salary figures, personal screening criteria, raw private messages. See the sanitisation rule in [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md). This repo teaches *how* the system works, never *what* anyone's search contains.
- **Bringing prior work?** Welcome. That's a goal of the Research stage. You're licensing the copy you contribute; you keep every independent right to your own original. You lose nothing by sharing it.

To assert this, **sign off your commits** with the Developer Certificate of Origin: `git commit -s` adds a `Signed-off-by:` line meaning "I have the right to contribute this." No forms, no signatures. (`/os-save` signs off for you.)

## What's licensed under what

- **Code and skills**: [Apache License 2.0](LICENSE). Permissive: anyone can use, modify, and build on it, including commercially.
- **Docs and written context** (`docs/`, `context/`, `build-log.md`, etc.): [Creative Commons Attribution 4.0](LICENSE-content) (CC-BY-4.0). Same freedom, with attribution required.

Both are permissive on purpose, so the community (or a future foundation, if this ever incorporates) can do whatever's needed later without chasing down every contributor for permission.

## Who "the community" is, honestly

A community isn't a legal entity, so the project is currently **stewarded by Simon Conway** on the community's behalf, under the open licences above. If it ever incorporates, the Apache-2.0 inbound licence makes moving the rights to that body painless. Nobody is quietly accumulating private ownership of your work. The licences are what guarantee it stays open.

## The one question that gates a merge

What does this let the team do now? If you can answer that, open the PR.
